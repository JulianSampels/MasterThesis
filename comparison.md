| Aspect                                       | Conventional Link Prediction                                                                              | from Entity to Fact Suggestion                                                                                                                             |
|----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Task definition**                          | Predict one missing element of a triple when the relation is already known, e.g., ((h,r,?)) or ((?,r,t)). | Starting from an anchor entity (h), generate and rank plausible complete fact candidates ((r,t)) without assuming that the relation is known in advance.   |
| **Primary goal**                             | Fill a predefined slot in a partially specified triple.                                                   | Suggest useful missing facts for an entity in a knowledge graph enrichment workflow.                                                                       |
| **Typical application**                      | Query completion, standard KG benchmarking, relation-specific fact completion.                            | Entity-page completion, KG curation, enterprise data enrichment, human-in-the-loop fact suggestion, exploratory KG analysis.                               |
| **Input**                                    | A partially specified triple with the relation fixed, e.g., ((h,r,?)).                                    | A single anchor entity (h), optionally together with its local graph context.                                                                              |
| **Output**                                   | A ranked list of candidate entities for the missing slot.                                                 | A ranked list of complete fact candidates ((r,t)), potentially spanning multiple relation types.                                                           |
| **Relation known at query time?**            | Yes.                                                                                                      | No.                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Prediction unit**                          | One missing entity under a fixed relation.                                                                | A complete fact suggestion consisting of both relation and tail entity.                                                                                    |
| **Output structure**                         | Single-slot ranking.                                                                                      | Structured fact ranking over heterogeneous relation–entity pairs.                                                                                          |
| **Search space**                             | Candidate entities for one fixed relation.                                                                | Joint search over candidate relations and candidate tail entities.                                                                                         |
| **Main challenge**                           | Rank the correct missing entity under a known semantic predicate.                                         | Discover relevant relation types, generate plausible relation–tail combinations, and rank complete facts efficiently.                                      |
| **Inference complexity**                     | Comparatively narrower; relation-conditioned ranking.                                                     | Larger and combinatorial; requires candidate generation, pruning, and reranking.                                                                           |
| **Evaluation focus**                         | MRR, Hits@K, relation-conditioned ranking quality.                                                        | Candidate efficiency, coverage, entity-level utility, and end-to-end tuple/fact quality.                                                                   |
| **User workflow assumption**                 | The user/system already knows which relation is missing.                                                  | The user/system starts from an entity and does not know which fact type is missing.                                                                        |
| **Why existing methods may be insufficient** | They assume the semantic target relation is provided.                                                     | They must be adapted to infer both relation and tail, often with poor efficiency or weak coverage-quality trade-offs.                                      |
| **Related work / research tradition**        | Extensive literature in knowledge graph completion and link prediction.                                   | Limited prior work under comparable settings; closest connections are edge-agnostic KGC, tuple prediction, entity-centric enrichment, and fact suggestion. |
